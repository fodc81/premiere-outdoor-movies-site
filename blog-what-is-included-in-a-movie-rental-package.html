What's Included in a Movie Rental Package?

By Premiere Outdoor Movies · June 29, 2026
Home› Blog› What's Included in a Movie Rental Package?

If you are comparing vendors and asking what is included in movie rental package options, you are really asking a bigger question: what will you need to handle yourself, and what will the company handle for you? That difference matters more than most planners expect. A low advertised price can sound great until you realize it only covers a screen and projector drop-off, while a full-service package includes the equipment, the crew, the setup, the operation during the event, and the teardown after the last credits roll.

For hosts planning a backyard birthday, a school movie night, a church gathering, or a large community event, the best package is usually the one that removes work from your plate. A movie night should feel easy for the organizer and polished for the audience. That is why understanding exactly what comes with the rental is one of the most important parts of choosing the right provider.

What is included in a movie rental package?

In most full-service movie rental packages, you are not just renting gear. You are booking a managed event experience. That usually includes the inflatable movie screen, projection system, professional sound, on-site crew, setup, live operation during the movie, and teardown.

That is the biggest distinction between a true event-production company and a basic equipment rental service. One gives you the tools. The other gives you a working movie event with professionals there to run it.

The exact package details can vary based on crowd size, venue conditions, and whether the event is indoors, outdoors, or drive-in style. Still, there are a few core components you should expect to see in a quality package.

A low advertised price only covers a drop-off. A full-service package covers the entire night — equipment, crew, setup, operation, and teardown.

The screen is only the starting point

The most visible part of any package is the movie screen. For residential events, that may be a smaller inflatable screen designed for neighborhood-friendly viewing. For schools, HOAs, municipalities, and festivals, the screen may be much larger to give everyone a clear view from a distance.

Screen size should match the audience, not just the budget. A screen that works well for 30 guests in a backyard may be completely inadequate for 300 people spread across a field. Good providers help you choose the correct screen based on attendance, viewing distance, and site layout.

Some packages also vary by aspect ratio or screen style depending on the content and the event environment. If you are hosting in a gym, auditorium, park, or parking lot, the recommended setup may look different. That is normal. The right package is not always the smallest or the biggest one. It is the one that fits your event correctly.

Projection equipment should be matched to the venue

A movie package should include a projector capable of delivering a bright, clear image for the setting. That sounds obvious, but it is where many DIY or low-cost rentals fall short. Not every projector performs well outdoors, and not every venue has the same lighting conditions.

For an evening backyard movie, projection needs are different than for a twilight community event or an indoor school function with ambient light. A professional package factors that in. The projector, lenses, cabling, and playback system should all be selected to work together.

You should also expect the provider to supply the content playback source as part of the presentation setup, whether that involves a media player, laptop, or other approved source. In some cases, movie licensing is separate from the equipment package, especially for public events. That is one of the most important things to clarify early, because the equipment package and the legal right to show a movie are not always the same line item.

Sound is a major part of what is included in movie rental package pricing

A movie night can survive a less-than-perfect seat arrangement. It cannot survive weak sound. If guests cannot hear the dialogue clearly, the event loses energy fast.

That is why professional speakers and audio support are typically included in a complete package. For small gatherings, that may mean a compact but powerful speaker system. For schools, churches, corporate events, and public screenings, the audio package may be scaled up significantly to cover a wider area.

The right sound setup depends on the audience size and the space itself. An open field, a courtyard, and a gym all behave differently. Strong providers account for that instead of bringing the same speaker pair to every job. If you are planning a drive-in style event, the audio solution may be different again, sometimes using radio transmission rather than traditional front-facing speakers.

Setup and teardown are where full-service value shows up

A lot of event planners focus on the equipment list first, but setup and teardown are often the most valuable part of the package. Inflatable screens, projectors, audio systems, power distribution, and signal lines all need to be installed properly. Then everything needs to be packed down safely once the event ends.

A full-service movie rental package typically includes delivery, setup before showtime, and teardown afterward. That means you are not left reading instructions, moving heavy equipment, or troubleshooting connections while guests arrive.

It also means the screen is anchored correctly, the projector is aligned, and the sound system is tested before the audience shows up. Those details are not glamorous, but they are exactly what make the event feel smooth.

On-site crew support keeps the event running

This is one of the most overlooked package elements, and one of the most valuable. A professional movie event should include trained crew members who stay on site to operate the equipment during the show.

That matters because live events do not always go perfectly. Weather shifts. Cables get bumped. Playback needs to be adjusted. Audio levels may need fine-tuning as the crowd settles in. When a crew is present, those issues get handled quickly and quietly.

For larger events, on-site support is even more important. Public screenings, school events, and festivals need operational oversight, not just equipment delivery. If the event is designed for hundreds or thousands of attendees, the production side should be treated seriously.

That is one reason many organizers prefer a company like Premiere Outdoor Movies rather than piecing together separate rentals on their own. Managed service reduces risk and gives you a clear point of accountability.

Power, space planning, and logistics may be included too

Some events have easy access to power. Others do not. Some venues are flat and simple. Others involve long cable runs, restricted access, or tight setup windows. A reliable provider looks at those details before event day.

Depending on the package and venue, planning support may include power assessment, equipment placement recommendations, setup timing, and guidance on audience layout. If your site requires a generator or special accommodations, that may be quoted separately rather than folded into the base package.

That is where the phrase it depends really applies. Two events with the same audience count can require different package pricing if one is in a simple backyard and the other is in a large public park with no nearby power source. The best vendors explain those differences clearly instead of hiding them.

What may not be included by default

When reviewing what is included in movie rental package options, it is just as important to ask what is not included. In many cases, the movie license for public performance is separate. That is common for schools, HOAs, cities, churches, and any event open beyond a private home audience.

You may also find that generators, stage platforms, custom branding, additional microphones, pre-show slides, or extra event hours are add-ons rather than standard inclusions. None of that is a problem, as long as the scope is explained upfront.

Residential private parties are often more straightforward. Large public events usually involve more customization. The key is transparency. You should know exactly what the package covers before you book.

How to tell if a package is right for your event

A good movie rental package should fit your audience size, venue, and event goals without making you manage production details yourself. If you are planning a simple backyard movie night, you probably do not need festival-scale equipment. If you are hosting a school field event or city movie series, you definitely need more than a screen and a speaker.

Ask practical questions. Who handles setup? Who stays during the event? Is sound included? What happens if the site needs extra power? Is licensing included or separate? Those answers tell you a lot about the provider.

The best package is not always the cheapest quote. It is the one that gives you confidence the event will actually work the way you expect it to.
