Finding extra time to spend with your young ones will make memories that will last a lifetime! Snuggling up under the stars for a special feature will make for the perfect evening. So find your favorite snacks, set up some cozy seating and gather your family and friends and their children for and prepare for an epic adventure that the kids will love and remember for years! Remember that finding the appropriate movie is up to you and what you feel is right for yourself and your audience. 5 Great Movie Options For Family Movie Night Toy Story. Toy Story is known as one of the best Pixar classics and is adored by millions of families. There are many different dynamics, ranging from sibling rivalry to toys being separated, making it a great movie to watch with your little ones to help them feel secure and to answer questions
  • Inside Out. This movie is wonderfully original and handles life lessons about growing up and handling emotions. The story is told through the mind of the 11-year-old perspective and shows that feelings and emotions are real, that children are not alone when they have ever-changing emotions and be useful to parents to remember as they navigate all of them.
  • Finding Nemo. While the beginning of the movie has moments that children may find intense, the story continues with messages of determination, the never-ending love of a parent and of loyalty. Ultimately, the movie may pose questions for small children but may also open the door for some great conversations about life and love.
  • Shrek. Shrek has an all-star cast, superb animation for its time and a bit of humor for all ages. The fairy tale takes you on a marvelous journey and has a happy ending that audiences want. Shrek has themes regarding believing in yourself, being loved for the person you really are, and good triumphing over evil.
  • The Incredibles. This is a smart, fresh and humorous movie about the real superheroes in life, the families who stick together. It can spark fun conversations with your children about what superhero they might wish to be like, dress like, and what super powers they might choose and how it might affect their life.

Life is busy and it can be difficult to carve out time for what matters most. To help to keep the fun and joy alive with our kids, prioritize adding in a planned family night!

Frequently Asked Questions

What movies are most popular for kids' outdoor movie nights?
Classic animated films, superhero movies, and family-friendly blockbusters are always crowd-pleasers. Popular choices include Moana, The Lion King, Frozen, Spider-Man, and Pixar favorites.
How late do kids' events usually run?
For younger audiences, we recommend scheduling the movie to end by 10 PM at the latest. Many families with toddlers choose earlier summer screenings that start around dusk (8–8:30 PM).
Is the sound system adjustable so it's not too loud for young kids?
Yes, our audio systems are fully adjustable. Our technicians calibrate volume to ensure it's comfortable for all audience members, including little ones.
Can we have activities or games before the movie?
Absolutely! Many of our clients set up yard games, face painting, or snack stations before the screening begins. We handle the movie setup so you can focus on the fun.
