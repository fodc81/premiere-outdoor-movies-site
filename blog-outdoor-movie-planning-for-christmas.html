Outdoor Movie Planning for Christmas

By Premiere Outdoor Movies · September 10, 2024
Home› Blog› Outdoor Movie Planning for Christmas

When the Christmas season comes around, it's the perfect time to gather your friends and family and watch a Christmas-themed movie outdoors. Planning an outdoor movie night takes some work, but with the proper preparation, you can create a memorable and festive event for everyone to enjoy.&nbsp;

If you're planning an outdoor movie night this Christmas, here are the supplies you'll need to succeed. Christmas is a magical time filled with joy, excitement, and fun. While there are many ways to celebrate the holidays, one of the most popular activities is to host an outdoor movie night. Whether you're looking to create a cozy atmosphere in your backyard or a significant event in the park, there are some steps to ensure your outdoor movie night is a hit.

The most essential item you'll need for your outdoor movie night is a projector. This will be what you use to project the movie onto the screen. You can rent a projector for the evening or buy one for yourself. If you're looking for a projector for a one-off event, you can find one for a reasonable price.

You'll need a screen to project the movie onto. The size of the screen you choose will depend on your viewing area. If you're hosting a small gathering, a smaller screen may suffice. You'll need a bigger screen if you're hosting a more significant conference. You can buy or rent a screen or even make one yourself.

To set the mood for your outdoor movie night, an audio system so everyone can hear the movie. You can rent a sound system or buy one for yourself. If you're buying one, make sure it's powerful enough to fill the viewing area with sound.

5. Pick a Date and Time:

Once you've chosen your movie, it's time to pick a date and time for your outdoor movie night. Remember the weather, as you don't want to be outside watching your movie in the rain or snow. Also, check your calendar to ensure you are distinct from other holiday events.&nbsp;

6. Send Out Invitations:

Now that you've chosen a movie and a date, it's time to let your friends and family know about your outdoor movie night. You can send out digital invitations or print out paper ones, and make sure to include the movie title, date, time, and location.

Snacks are essential for any outdoor movie night, and Popcorn is a classic favorite and can be easily made in large batches. Drinks are also necessary, so make sure you have enough for everyone. Consider providing various options, such as sodas, water, and hot chocolate.

8. Choose the Location:

Once you've decided on a movie, it's time to pick the location. If you're looking for a cozy atmosphere, your backyard is ideal. However, consider a park or other public space if you're looking for a more significant event. Make sure to factor in any permits or regulations you need to secure.

9. Make a Movie Schedule:

Creating a timeline for your outdoor movie night is essential factor in time for food and drink, which can be quickly done with a potluck style. Plan for enough time to cook and serve the food if you're providing the food. You'll also need to factor in the time for the movie to begin and end.

Consider decorating the space with lights and props to create a festive atmosphere. String lights around the area create a cozy atmosphere and add support to give the site a festive feel.

11. Create a Backup Plan:

It's always a good idea to create a backup plan in case something doesn't go as planned. Have a backup movie ready if the one you picked doesn't work. And have an alternate location in case the weather isn't cooperating.

12. Enjoy the show by taking pictures:

When your outdoor movie night is ready, it's time to enjoy the show! Make sure to have some fun and take plenty of pictures. Frequently Asked Questions

Can you host an outdoor movie night in cold weather?
Absolutely. With the right preparation — warm blankets, a fire pit, and hot cocoa — cold-weather screenings can be incredibly cozy. We recommend temperatures above 35°F for comfortable outdoor viewing.
What wind speeds are safe for an inflatable movie screen?
Our inflatable screens are anchored and rated for winds up to 15–20 mph. We monitor forecasts and will advise if conditions may be unsafe.
When does it get dark enough for an outdoor movie?
Darkness varies by season. In summer, you may need to wait until 8:30–9 PM for full darkness. In fall and winter, darkness arrives earlier, making for earlier screenings.
Do you offer winter outdoor movie packages?
Yes! Winter screenings can be magical. We provide reliable cold-weather equipment and recommend cozy additions like heated seating areas and warm refreshments.
