A Few Things You'll Need to Handle

  • Your content — You supply the movie. DVD via our included Denon player, or a downloaded file from a device with HDMI output. Public screenings of commercial films may require a public performance license.
  • Streaming content — Streaming requires a strong, reliable WiFi or hotspot signal at the venue. At larger outdoor venues, cellular or hotspot signals can be inconsistent — we recommend testing ahead of time or using a downloaded file.
  • Power access — We need two standard electrical outlets accessible from the setup area (one for the screen/projector, one for the audio system). For remote venues, a generator can be arranged — just mention it when booking.
  • Venue permits — Public parks, municipal grounds, and school fields may require event permits and/or a public performance license for commercial film screenings. We recommend confirming with your local authority well in advance.

Common Questions

Blockbuster Event FAQ

How much space does a 20-foot screen need?

+
The AirScreen 20 is 23 feet wide overall and nearly 19 feet tall. You'll need at least 40–60 feet of clearance in front for seating, plus space behind and to the sides for anchoring and guy-wire stakes. Athletic fields, park lawns, and festival grounds are ideal. Our team will assess your specific venue layout when you book.

What makes the audio different on this package?

+
The Blockbuster Event includes two Electro-Voice 1500-watt powered speakers — the same type used in professional live event production. This setup delivers clear, even sound across a 500-person crowd outdoors, with enough headroom to handle dialogue, music, and effects without distortion. It's a significant step up from the audio included in our 12-foot and 16-foot packages.

Do I need a permit for a 500-person outdoor movie event?

+
For events of this size at public venues, permits are almost always required — both for the gathering and for the commercial film screening itself. You'll want to check with your local parks department or municipal authority, and secure a public performance license through a service like Swank Motion Pictures or Criterion Pictures. We're happy to point you in the right direction when you book.

How does this compare to the Premiere Event (16-foot) package?

+
The Blockbuster steps up in three key ways: a larger screen (20 vs. 16 feet, roughly 70% more screen area), a more powerful projector (8,000 vs. standard lumens), and a professional dual-speaker audio system rated for 500-person crowds. If you're expecting 250 or fewer guests, the Premiere Event is the right fit. Above that, the Blockbuster is built for it.

Can you set up at a venue without power?

+
Yes — we can arrange a generator for venues without accessible power. Note that the Blockbuster package draws more power than smaller packages (screen blower plus projector plus dual speakers), so a properly sized generator is important. Just let us know when requesting your quote and we'll factor it in.

How far in advance should I book a 500-person event?

+
We recommend 4–6 weeks for events of this size, especially during peak season (May through September). Larger events benefit from the additional lead time to coordinate venue access, power setup, and permitting. That said, reach out regardless — we'll always do our best to accommodate your timeline.
