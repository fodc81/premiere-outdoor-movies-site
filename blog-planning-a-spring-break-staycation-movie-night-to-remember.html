When choosing to stay home for spring break there are friends and family that will be in the same situation of staying home but also looking for fun activities! Print up a quick and inexpensive invitation or, better yet, simply sent electronic invitations.
  • Make Your Own Pizza Night. Don't stress about the numerous choices or the expense of buying fancy foods for your guests. Instead, make a quick trip to the store and choose some pre-made dough. Also, choose your favorite sauce, cheeses, and fun toppings. Set up a station for friends and family to assemble their choice of pizza to enjoy and bake in shifts. As they are done baking, each pizza can then become a tasting pizza for everyone to try everyone's favorite! Putting together a simple tossed salad with other easy to grab snacks will keep your guests munching while the pizzas bake!
  • Activities. Since a 'staycation&quot; means that you have time to enjoy at home, make a full day of the fun and end the evening with the movie. This means keeping young children busy. Assemble a crafts table for drawing or game playing and don't forget a scavenger hunt to keep it exciting. Nothing makes memories like a strategically placed scavenger hunt!
  • Drinks. Depending on the budget you have chosen, drink options can be as simple or adventurous and you would like. A batch of a signature cocktail is always a fun idea but you can also stop at a bulk food store and pick up cans or bottles of your favorite beer, soda, or juices. One thing to be sure you have available and plentiful is water.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you host an outdoor movie night in cold weather?
Absolutely. With the right preparation — warm blankets, a fire pit, and hot cocoa — cold-weather screenings can be incredibly cozy. We recommend temperatures above 35°F for comfortable outdoor viewing.
What wind speeds are safe for an inflatable movie screen?
Our inflatable screens are anchored and rated for winds up to 15–20 mph. We monitor forecasts and will advise if conditions may be unsafe.
When does it get dark enough for an outdoor movie?
Darkness varies by season. In summer, you may need to wait until 8:30–9 PM for full darkness. In fall and winter, darkness arrives earlier, making for earlier screenings.
Do you offer winter outdoor movie packages?
Yes! Winter screenings can be magical. We provide reliable cold-weather equipment and recommend cozy additions like heated seating areas and warm refreshments.
