Planning Tips for Your Outdoor Movie Event

After more than 15 years and thousands of outdoor cinema events nationwide, we've learned a few things about what makes them go smoothly. Whether you're a first-timer or an annual HOA planner, these tips will help you get the most out of your event.

Book early, especially for summer. Spring and summer weekends fill up fast — sometimes months in advance in our busiest markets. If you have a date in mind, reach out as soon as possible to lock it in.

Think about your surface. Grass is ideal, but we regularly set up in parking lots, on courts, and on other hard surfaces with the right equipment. Let us know your venue type when you get in touch and we'll plan accordingly.

Pick a movie your audience will love — not just you. For HOA and community events, crowd-pleasing family films almost always outperform niche picks. For corporate events, comedies and crowd favorites tend to keep energy high. We're happy to make suggestions based on your audience.

Plan for ambient light. Screenings work best starting at or after dusk. If your event is in late spring or summer, plan for a later start time — around 8:30 or 9:00 PM — so the sky is dark enough for the best picture quality.

Have a rain plan. Your free weekday rain date is there for a reason. Communicate it clearly to your guests in advance so everyone knows the backup plan.
