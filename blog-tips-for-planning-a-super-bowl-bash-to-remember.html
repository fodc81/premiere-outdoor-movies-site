That&rsquo;s not to say everything needs to be perfect. After all, someone is bound to spill a beverage and or be unhappy that their team isn't winning. Take care of the most important components and you will have yourself a great event! Tips For Planning A Super Bowl Bash To Remember Food. The first decision will be whether you choose to provide the food items or have it be a combination of dishes that your guests will contribute as well. Either way, a wide variety of easy to eat foods usually works best. If your guests are contributing then try to be organized with who is bringing what. This prevents 10 different types of nachos from being the only food you end up with. If you are ordering take out, decide which variety of food and how much you will need. If you are cooking yourself, plan recipes and gather the ingredients you will need ahead of the big day.
  • Drinks. Trying to decide on the best drinks to have on hand can be mind-boggling. It is best to offer a few types of both beer and wine, plus a little something for the cocktail-drinking crew. Don't forget to have an abundance of water, juices and soft drinks for children that are invited as well as those who don't drink alcohol. Running out of ice is always a dilemma so plan to buy and store some in coolers so that you are prepared.
  • Decorations . This is, of course, an optional step. Not every Super Bowl party needs decorations but it is an easy and fun way to create a festive atmosphere. Check out your local party store and browse through the seasonal Super Bowl section that should be out this time of year. Grab some banners, some streamers and anything else you think might help spice up your party space.
  • Big Screen Viewing. It's obvious that that the most important part of any Super Bowl party is the Super Bowl itself. You and your guests are going to want to watch the game in style, this means providing a big screen for easy viewing from any seat. Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book an outdoor movie event?
We recommend booking at least 2–4 weeks in advance, especially during spring and summer peak season. Last-minute bookings may be accommodated depending on availability, so it's always worth reaching out.
What size screen do I need for my event?
Screen size depends on audience size and viewing distance. A 12–16 ft screen works for 50–100 guests; a 20 ft screen suits 100–200; for larger crowds, 24 ft and up is ideal. We help you choose the right size during your consultation.
What happens if it rains on the day of my event?
We monitor weather closely and communicate with you beforehand. Many of our screens are rated for light wind, and we can often reschedule at no extra charge if severe weather forces a cancellation.
Do you provide the projector, screen, and sound system?
Yes. Our packages include everything: the inflatable screen, high-lumen projector, speakers, and a technician for setup and operation throughout your event.
