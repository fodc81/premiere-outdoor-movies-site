FAQ

Questions About the Ultimate Event

How far behind the crowd does the projector need to be?

+
For our 30 and 40-foot screens, projectors typically need 80–120 feet of throw distance. For the 52-foot screen, allow for 100–150 feet. Our team assesses the venue during site planning and confirms the exact setup. The projector is always positioned well behind the last row of the audience and aimed over the crowd — no obstructions, clean sight lines throughout.

Is a generator always required for the Ultimate Event?

+
Yes, and it's always included. Screens of this size require substantial power for the inflatable blowers, the high-lumen laser projector, the speaker array, and all support equipment. Venue power sources are rarely adequate or conveniently located for large outdoor deployments. We bring industrial generator power sized for the full system load — it's part of the package.

How long does setup take for a 40 or 52-foot screen?

+
Typical setup time for the 30-foot screen is 2–3 hours. The 40-foot requires 3–4 hours and the 52-foot requires 4–5 hours or more depending on site conditions. We always plan for early access and build in extra time to test and calibrate before the first guest arrives. For major events, we may coordinate a pre-event site visit as well.

What does professional video switching allow me to do?

+
Video switching lets you run a full multi-source program on the big screen — trailers, sponsor reels, countdown timers, live camera feeds, and your main feature, all seamlessly transitioned. You can cut between sources instantly with no dead air. This is essential for sponsored events or festivals where the screen carries advertising or programming before and between features.

Do I need a special permit to show a movie to a large crowd?

+
For public screenings — any event where the public is admitted, whether free or paid — a public performance license is required for commercially released films. Premiere Outdoor Movies does not provide these licenses, but services like Swank Motion Pictures or Criterion Pictures make the process straightforward and affordable. We can point you in the right direction. Private events (company picnics, invite-only gatherings) typically do not require a license.

Can the 52-foot screen handle a windy event?

+
The AirScreen 52 is engineered to handle outdoor conditions, but all large screens require proper anchoring and favorable weather. Our heavy-duty tiedown systems are sized for each screen and installed to manufacturer specifications. For all events, we monitor weather conditions in the days leading up and maintain direct communication with you about any concerns. Sustained winds over 25–30 mph typically require operational adjustments or postponement for the largest screens — your safety and your audience's safety always comes first.
